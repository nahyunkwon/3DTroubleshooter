Toolhead

The 3D printing Toolhead allows you to 3D print objects using the FFD (fuse filament deposition) method. This method consists in thermo extrude plastic filament through a hot-end.

Related terms: extruder, hotend, nozzle, print head

Slow down the printing speed. I find all jerk settings should be 10mm/s and master print speed shouldn't really go over 40mm/s if you want a quality print on *most* machines. It may also be prudent to check and possibly reduce acceleration speeds.
Belt is too loose/tight.
Check your x and y axis belts. When perfectly tensioned, they twang somewhat like a guitar string. If under tensioned, you'll get lines like these. If over tensioned, you could have some defects in prints but the danger is mostly to the motor and belt itself. There are videos you can watch for this, as well as belt tensioners you can print.
Set-screw is too loose, not tightened well / pulley loose
Not enough electrical current getting to the motors
